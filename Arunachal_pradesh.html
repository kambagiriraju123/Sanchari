ARUNACHAL PRADESH

Arunachal Pradesh is a state located in the northeastern region of India. It is the easternmost state of India and is bordered by Bhutan to the west, Tibet to the north, Myanmar to the east, and the Indian states of Assam and Nagaland to the south. The state covers an area of 83,743 square kilometers and has a population of around 1.4 million people. It is known for its rich biodiversity, scenic landscapes, and diverse cultural heritage. The official language of Arunachal Pradesh is English, while Hindi and other regional languages are also widely spoken.

The state's economy is primarily based on agriculture and forestry, with rice, maize, millets, and pulses being the main crops grown in the region. The state also has significant potential for hydropower generation and has several dams and power projects in the region. Arunachal Pradesh has several popular tourist destinations, including Tawang, which is known for its ancient monasteries and scenic landscapes.
