ASSAM

Assam is a state located in the northeastern part of India. It is bordered by Bhutan and Arunachal Pradesh to the north, Nagaland and Manipur to the east, Meghalaya to the south, and West Bengal and Bangladesh to the west. The state has an area of 78,438 square kilometers and a population of approximately 35 million people. Assam is known for its tea gardens, wildlife sanctuaries, ancient temples, and cultural heritage. The state is also home to several indigenous tribes, each with its own distinct culture and language. Assamese is the official language of the state, but many other languages, including Bengali, Hindi, and English, are also spoken.

Assam is home to several wildlife sanctuaries and national parks, including the Kaziranga National Park, which is known for its population of the one-horned rhinoceros. The state also has several ancient temples, including the Kamakhya Temple, which is considered one of the most important shrines in Hinduism. Guwahati, the largest city in Assam, is known for its ancient temples, museums, and markets. Other popular tourist destinations in the state include Majuli, the largest river island in the world, and the Manas National Park, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
