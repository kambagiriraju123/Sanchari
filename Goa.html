GOA

Goa is a state located on the western coast of India, bordering the Arabian Sea. The state has an area of 3,702 square kilometers and a population of approximately 1.5 million people. It is the smallest state in India by area and the fourth smallest by population. Goa was a Portuguese colony until 1961, and as a result, the state has a unique blend of Indian and Portuguese cultures. The official language of Goa is Konkani, although English and Hindi are also widely spoken.

Goa is known for its beautiful beaches, picturesque landscapes, and rich cultural heritage. The state is home to several ancient temples, churches, and forts, including the Basilica of Bom Jesus, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The state's economy is primarily based on tourism, with millions of tourists visiting the state every year to enjoy its beaches, water sports, and nightlife.
