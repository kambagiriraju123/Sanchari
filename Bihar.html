BIHAR

Bihar is a state located in the eastern part of India. It is bordered by Nepal to the north, West Bengal to the east, Jharkhand to the south, and Uttar Pradesh to the west. The state has an area of 94,163 square kilometers and a population of approximately 121 million people. Bihar has a rich cultural heritage and is known for its historical sites and ancient monuments. The state was an important center of power during the Mauryan Empire, which ruled over much of India from the 3rd century BCE to the 3rd century CE. The state is also known for its contributions to the fields of science, mathematics, and literature during the ancient period. The official language of Bihar is Hindi, although several regional languages, including Bhojpuri, Maithili, and Magahi, are also widely spoken. The state has a diverse population, with several indigenous tribes living in the region.

Bihar has several important historical sites and ancient monuments, including the ruins of the ancient city of Nalanda, which was an important center of learning in ancient India. Other popular tourist destinations in the state include Bodh Gaya, the site where the Buddha is said to have attained enlightenment, and Rajgir, which was the capital of the ancient Magadha Empire.
