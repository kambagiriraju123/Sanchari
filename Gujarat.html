GUJARAT

Gujarat is a state located in the western part of India, bordered by the Arabian Sea to the west and the states of Rajasthan to the north, Madhya Pradesh to the east, and Maharashtra to the south. The state has an area of 196,024 square kilometers and a population of approximately 63 million people. The official language of Gujarat is Gujarati, although Hindi and English are also widely spoken. Gujarat is known for its rich cultural heritage, including its vibrant folk traditions, music, and dance forms. The state is also home to several ancient temples, mosques, and monuments, including the Somnath Temple, which is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas in Hinduism.

Gandhinagar is the capital of Gujarat, and other major cities in the state include Ahmedabad, Surat, Vadodara, and Rajkot. In recent years, the state has seen significant infrastructure development, including the construction of new airports, highways, and railways, which have helped to boost the state's economy and tourism.
