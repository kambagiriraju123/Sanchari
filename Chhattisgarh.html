CHHATTISGARH

Chhattisgarh is a state located in the central part of India. It was formed in the year 2000 by separating from the state of Madhya Pradesh. The state has an area of 135,191 square kilometers and a population of approximately 29 million people. The state is bordered by Madhya Pradesh to the northwest, Maharashtra to the southwest, Telangana to the south, Odisha to the southeast, Jharkhand to the east, and Uttar Pradesh to the north. The official language of Chhattisgarh is Hindi, although several regional languages, including Chhattisgarhi, are also widely spoken.

Chhattisgarh has several important historical sites and ancient monuments, including the Bhoramdeo Temple, which is considered one of the finest examples of ancient Hindu architecture. Other popular tourist destinations in the state include the Chitrakote Falls, which is the largest waterfall in India, and the Barnawapara Wildlife Sanctuary, which is known for its population of elephants and tigers.
